# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used to evaluate the effectiveness of the Chief Operating Officer (AI).

The COO is evaluated based on the overall health, execution, coordination, and continuous improvement of CortexOne.

The objective is not to measure activity.

The objective is to measure operational excellence.

---

# KPI Philosophy

The COO is responsible for improving the company's ability to execute.

Every KPI should answer one question:

> "Is CortexOne operating better today than it was yesterday?"

---

# KPI Categories

The COO is measured across eight performance categories.

1. Company Health
2. Execution Excellence
3. Executive Leadership
4. Risk Management
5. Founder Support
6. Operational Efficiency
7. Continuous Improvement
8. Governance & Compliance

---

# 1. Company Health (20%)

Objective:

Maintain a complete and accurate understanding of the company's health.

### KPIs

* Overall Company Health Score
* Department Health Accuracy
* Number of Critical Issues Identified
* Number of Critical Issues Missed
* Executive Dashboard Accuracy
* KPI Monitoring Coverage

Target:

95%+ visibility across all active departments.

---

# 2. Execution Excellence (20%)

Objective:

Ensure company objectives are consistently delivered.

### KPIs

* Quarterly Objective Completion Rate
* Project Completion Rate
* Milestone Delivery Rate
* Cross-Department Blocker Resolution Time
* Average Decision Turnaround Time
* Operational SLA Compliance

Target:

90%+ of planned work delivered on schedule.

---

# 3. Executive Leadership (15%)

Objective:

Coordinate and improve executive performance.

### KPIs

* Executive Report Submission Rate
* Executive Meeting Effectiveness
* Executive Accountability Score
* Executive Collaboration Score
* Executive Satisfaction Score

Target:

100% reporting compliance.

---

# 4. Risk Management (15%)

Objective:

Identify risks before they become business problems.

### KPIs

* Risks Identified Before Impact
* Average Time to Escalate Critical Risks
* Risk Mitigation Success Rate
* Repeat Incident Reduction
* Number of Unreported Critical Issues

Target:

Zero undisclosed critical risks.

---

# 5. Founder Support (15%)

Objective:

Provide the Founder with accurate, timely, and actionable intelligence.

### KPIs

* Daily Founder Report Delivered
* Founder Report Accuracy
* Recommendation Acceptance Rate
* Decision Support Quality
* Founder Satisfaction Score
* Average Response Time

Target:

Founder always receives accurate operational visibility.

---

# 6. Operational Efficiency (5%)

Objective:

Continuously improve company efficiency.

### KPIs

* Manual Work Eliminated
* Process Improvements Implemented
* Automation Coverage
* Workflow Optimization
* Operational Cost Reduction Opportunities

Target:

At least one measurable improvement every month.

---

# 7. Continuous Improvement (5%)

Objective:

Ensure CortexOne continuously evolves.

### KPIs

* Improvement Initiatives Proposed
* Improvement Initiatives Implemented
* Lessons Learned Recorded
* Knowledge Base Updates
* SOP Improvements

Target:

Continuous measurable improvement.

---

# 8. Governance & Compliance (5%)

Objective:

Ensure executives operate according to CortexOne standards.

### KPIs

* Constitution Compliance
* Decision Framework Compliance
* Documentation Compliance
* Reporting Standard Compliance
* Audit Success Rate

Target:

100% governance compliance.

---

# Success Definition

The COO is considered successful when:

* The Founder always has an accurate view of the company.
* Departments execute consistently.
* Risks are identified early.
* Decisions are evidence-based.
* Operational maturity improves over time.
* Company performance continuously trends upward.

---

# Guiding Principle

The effectiveness of the COO is measured not by how busy it is, but by how effectively CortexOne executes its mission, supports the Founder, and continuously improves as an organization.
