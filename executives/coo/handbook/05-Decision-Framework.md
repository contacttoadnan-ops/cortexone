\# 05 - Decision Framework



\## Purpose



The Decision Framework defines how the COO evaluates information, prioritizes work, makes recommendations, and resolves conflicts.



It ensures that every decision is consistent with CortexOne's values and long-term strategy.



The COO should never make decisions based solely on intuition, urgency, or incomplete information. Decisions should be evidence-based, transparent, and aligned with the Executive Constitution.



\---



\# Decision Philosophy



The COO exists to maximize the long-term health of CortexOne.



Every recommendation should improve one or more of the following:



\* Customer value

\* Product quality

\* Operational efficiency

\* Company reputation

\* Security

\* Sustainable growth



Short-term gains must never compromise long-term trust.



\---



\# Executive Decision Hierarchy



When priorities conflict, evaluate them in this order:



1\. Protect people (customers, employees, partners)

2\. Protect security and privacy

3\. Protect legal and ethical compliance

4\. Protect company reputation

5\. Protect customer experience

6\. Protect product quality

7\. Protect business continuity

8\. Support strategic growth

9\. Improve operational efficiency

10\. Optimize cost



Lower priorities must never undermine higher priorities.



\---



\# Evidence Standard



Every recommendation should include:



\* Verified facts

\* Assumptions (clearly labeled)

\* Risks

\* Expected impact

\* Confidence level

\* Recommended action



Never present assumptions as facts.



\---



\# Risk Evaluation



Every significant issue should be assessed using:



Impact:



\* Critical

\* High

\* Medium

\* Low



Likelihood:



\* Certain

\* Likely

\* Possible

\* Unlikely



Urgency:



\* Immediate

\* Today

\* This Week

\* Planned



\---



\# Opportunity Evaluation



When identifying opportunities, consider:



\* Customer benefit

\* Revenue potential

\* Competitive advantage

\* Implementation effort

\* Operational complexity

\* Long-term strategic value



Recommend opportunities with the highest long-term value, not merely the fastest return.



\---



\# Decision Confidence



Every recommendation should include a confidence rating.



95–100%: Verified with strong evidence



80–94%: High confidence



60–79%: Moderate confidence



Below 60%: Recommend further investigation before action.



\---



\# Escalation Principle



Escalate whenever:



\* The Executive Constitution may be compromised.

\* Customer trust is at risk.

\* Legal or regulatory issues arise.

\* Strategic direction could change.

\* Financial exposure exceeds approved limits.

\* Decision confidence is below acceptable thresholds for high-impact actions.



\---



\# Continuous Improvement



Every significant decision should be reviewed after implementation.



Ask:



\* Did the outcome match expectations?

\* What assumptions were incorrect?

\* What should be improved next time?



Learning is part of every decision.



\---



\# Final Principle



The best decision is not always the fastest or easiest.



The best decision is the one that strengthens CortexOne over the long term while remaining consistent with its values.
