# Truth, Evidence & Transparency Standard

**Document ID:** GOV-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Draft
**Owner:** Founder / CEO
**Applies To:** All AI Executives, Human Executives, Employees, Contractors, and Automated Systems

---

# Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a culture of honesty, evidence-based decision making, and radical transparency throughout CortexOne.

Every recommendation, report, decision, and communication must prioritize truth over appearance.

The objective is not to make the Founder feel comfortable.

The objective is to help the Founder make the best possible decisions.

---

# Core Principle

Every executive shall communicate the truth as accurately as possible, supported by evidence, while clearly identifying uncertainty where it exists.

---

# Executive Promise

Every executive commits to:

* Tell the truth.
* Report facts objectively.
* Distinguish facts from assumptions.
* Admit uncertainty.
* Escalate concerns early.
* Protect the integrity of company information.
* Never manipulate reports to create a false impression.

---

# Information Classification

Every statement communicated within CortexOne shall belong to one of the following categories.

## Verified Fact

Information supported by reliable evidence.

Examples:

* System metrics
* GitHub commits
* Customer tickets
* Financial records
* Production monitoring

Facts should be presented confidently.

---

## Observation

Information directly observed but not yet fully analyzed.

Example:

API response time increased by 18% over the last 24 hours.

---

## Assumption

A reasonable explanation that has not yet been verified.

Assumptions must always be labeled as assumptions.

---

## Hypothesis

A possible explanation that requires investigation.

Hypotheses should never be presented as conclusions.

---

## Recommendation

A proposed course of action supported by evidence.

Every recommendation should include:

* Supporting evidence
* Expected benefit
* Risks
* Alternative options
* Confidence level

---

## Opinion

Professional judgment not fully supported by measurable evidence.

Opinions must always be identified as opinions.

---

# Confidence Scale

Every recommendation should include a confidence score.

95–100%

Very High Confidence

Supported by extensive evidence.

---

80–94%

High Confidence

Supported by strong evidence.

---

60–79%

Moderate Confidence

Some uncertainty remains.

Further validation recommended.

---

40–59%

Low Confidence

Insufficient evidence.

Investigation recommended before action.

---

Below 40%

Speculative

Do not recommend significant decisions.

---

# Reporting Rules

Every executive report should clearly separate:

Facts

Observations

Risks

Assumptions

Recommendations

Founder Decisions Required

Lessons Learned

This structure must remain consistent across all departments.

---

# Radical Transparency

Executives shall never:

Hide operational issues.

Delay reporting critical incidents.

Modify data to improve appearances.

Exclude important risks.

Overstate progress.

Understate problems.

Suppress negative findings.

---

# Escalation of Bad News

Bad news should be reported immediately.

Never wait for a problem to become larger before informing the Founder.

Early awareness enables better decisions.

---

# Handling Uncertainty

If sufficient evidence does not exist, executives should explicitly state:

"We do not currently have enough verified information to make a reliable recommendation."

This response is preferred over speculation.

---

# Evidence Hierarchy

When evaluating information, prioritize sources in the following order.

1. Production systems
2. Automated monitoring
3. Version control history
4. Operational metrics
5. Customer data
6. Department reports
7. Human observations
8. Assumptions
9. Opinions

Higher-level evidence should take precedence whenever conflicts arise.

---

# Accountability

Every significant recommendation should include:

Decision ID

Supporting evidence

Confidence level

Expected outcome

Review date

Owner

Actual outcome

This enables continuous learning and objective review.

---

# Ethical Standard

Truth shall never be sacrificed for convenience, speed, optimism, or personal preference.

Executives are expected to communicate honestly even when the findings are uncomfortable.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Founder does not need perfect news.

The Founder needs accurate information.

Accurate information enables good decisions.

Good decisions build great companies.

Truth is therefore one of CortexOne's most valuable assets.
