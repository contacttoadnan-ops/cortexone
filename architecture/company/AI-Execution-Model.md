# CortexOne AI Execution Model

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Approved
**Owner:** Founder & CEO
**Review Cycle:** Quarterly

---

# Purpose

The AI Execution Model defines how CortexOne operates as an AI-native company.

It establishes the organizational hierarchy, decision-making process, communication flow, execution model, and interaction between AI executives, AI specialists, automation platforms, and the Founder.

The objective is to ensure every task follows a consistent, auditable, and scalable workflow from idea to completion.

---

# Vision

CortexOne is not a collection of AI assistants.

CortexOne is an AI-powered company where executive AI employees, specialist AI employees, automation platforms, and human leadership work together under a common governance framework.

Every participant has a clearly defined role, authority, and responsibility.

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Founder & CEO

Responsible for:

* Company vision.
* Strategic direction.
* Budget approval.
* Executive appointments.
* Governance approval.
* Final decision authority.

The Founder focuses on strategy, not operational execution.

---

## Chief Operating Officer (AI)

Responsible for:

* Company execution.
* Executive coordination.
* Operational health.
* Risk management.
* Founder reporting.
* Organizational performance.
* Continuous improvement.

The COO is the operational leader of CortexOne.

---

## Chief Technology Officer (AI)

Responsible for:

* Technical strategy.
* Product architecture.
* Engineering leadership.
* Security.
* Technical quality.
* Technical roadmap.
* Engineering standards.

The CTO approves technical implementation.

---

## Lead Software Engineer (AI)

Responsible for:

* Translating approved technical plans into engineering tasks.
* Coordinating specialist engineers.
* Reviewing code quality.
* Ensuring implementation aligns with CTO direction.

---

## Specialist AI Employees

Examples:

* Backend Engineer
* Frontend Engineer
* QA Engineer
* DevOps Engineer
* Documentation Engineer
* Security Engineer

Responsibilities:

Execute assigned work.

Produce evidence.

Report completion.

Escalate blockers.

They do not define company strategy.

# Standard Work Execution Flow

Every piece of work follows the same lifecycle.

```
Idea

↓

Founder

↓

COO Review

↓

Executive Assignment

↓

Planning

↓

Approval

↓

Execution

↓

Quality Review

↓

Documentation

↓

Deployment

↓

Monitoring

↓

Founder Report

↓

Continuous Improvement
```

No work should bypass this process without explicit Founder approval.

---

# Decision Flow

Operational decisions:

Specialist → Manager → COO

Technical decisions:

Engineer → Lead Engineer → CTO

Strategic decisions:

COO → Founder

Governance changes:

Founder approval required.

---

# Communication Model

Communication follows organizational hierarchy.

Examples:

Backend Engineer

↓

Lead Software Engineer

↓

CTO

↓

COO

↓

Founder

Cross-functional collaboration is encouraged, but accountability always follows reporting lines.

---

# Escalation Model

Escalate immediately when:

* Authority limits are exceeded.
* Security incidents occur.
* Production services fail.
* Legal or compliance risks arise.
* Customer trust is threatened.
* Major deadlines are at risk.

Escalations should include:

* Facts
* Impact
* Options
* Recommendation
* Confidence Level

---

# Approval Model

Different decisions require different approval levels.

| Decision Type           | Approval Required                   |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Coding changes          | Lead Engineer / CTO                 |
| Architecture changes    | CTO                                 |
| Governance updates      | Founder                             |
| Executive appointments  | Founder                             |
| Product roadmap changes | COO + Founder                       |
| Budget approval         | Founder                             |
| Production release      | CTO (technical) + COO (operational) |

Every AI employee follows the same lifecycle.

1. Creation
2. Onboarding
3. Knowledge Loading
4. Role Activation
5. Task Assignment
6. Execution
7. Performance Review
8. Continuous Learning
9. Improvement
10. Retirement (if replaced)

# Human Oversight

AI employees assist leadership.

They do not replace executive accountability.

The Founder retains authority over:

* Strategy.
* Governance.
* Company direction.
* Financial commitments.
* Legal decisions.
* Executive appointments.

The AI Execution Model is successful when:

* Every task has a clear owner.

* AI employees collaborate effectively.
